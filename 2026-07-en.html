Dear colleagues,

With the summer holidays just around the corner, we would like to update you on the latest developments through this new sustainability newsletter.

We are operating in a period of significant change. Rising costs continue to put pressure on sustainable choices. Consumers still say that sustainability is important to them, but their willingness to pay for it is declining. Whereas a few years ago this was around 60–70%, it is now closer to 50% or even lower.

Sustainability, however, is not moving into the background. On the contrary, it is increasingly becoming a fundamental requirement for the way we operate, driven by sustainability legislation and growing customer demand. New EU legislation is on the horizon: PPWR (Packaging and Packaging Waste Regulation), EmpCo (green claims) and the Forced Labour Regulation (with more to come). Transparency requirements are also continuing to increase, particularly through CSRD and footprinting. In addition, we are facing new costs, for example through transport and packaging levies, while customers are asking more targeted questions and increasingly expect evidence to support our claims, as demonstrated recently by Lidl’s questions about our human rights approach. In short, sustainability directly influences how we manage risks, create value and improve efficiency.

Through IMPACT2030, we are helping to build a future-proof floriculture sector. We are embedding sustainability even further into the core of our business, not as an additional activity, but as an integral part of the way we work. The taskforces are now up and running, helping us to create focus and accelerate progress across the themes of carbon, circular packaging & zero waste, social impact and crop protection. We look forward to sharing more after the summer.

Have a great summer!
Linda van Waveren
Group Sustainability Manager
Dutch Flower Group

Be careful with sustainability claims
By Linda van Waveren

From September 2026, the European EmpCo Directive will come into force. This legislation introduces stricter requirements for sustainability claims and is intended to prevent misleading practices (greenwashing). Several customers are already raising questions about EmpCo.

By Linda van Waveren
